# Experiment 02 – 8×4.5 vs 10×4.5 Propeller Comparison

## Objective

The objective of this experiment was to compare the flight characteristics of 8×4.5 and 10×4.5 propellers using the same flight controller, motors, ESCs, battery, and airframe.

The comparison focused on stability, responsiveness, thrust, oscillation behaviour, and PID tuning requirements.

---

# Test Platform

Frame:
- F450 Quadcopter Frame

Flight Controller:
- Arduino r3
- MPU6050 IMU

Motors:
- A2212 1000KV Brushless Motors

ESC:
- 30A ESC

Battery:
- 3S LiPo

---

# Comparison

| Parameter | 8×4.5 | 10×4.5 |
|------------|--------|---------|
| Hover Stability | Excellent | Excellent (after tuning) |
| Lift | Moderate | High |
| Throttle Required | Higher | Lower |
| Efficiency | Moderate | Better |
| Stick Response | Fast | Slightly smoother |
| Oscillation Tendency | Low | High before tuning |
| Recovery | Excellent | Excellent after tuning |
| PID Tuning Required | Minor | Significant |
| Overall Stability | Excellent | Excellent after tuning |

---

# 8×4.5 Behaviour

The 8×4.5 propellers served as the baseline configuration.

After correcting the accelerometer offset bcz it going in the front left direction, the quadcopter demonstrated:

- Stable hover
- Smooth control
- Minimal oscillations
- Reliable recovery after stick inputs
- Predictable flight behaviour

The firmware became the reference configuration for future development.

---

# 10×4.5 Behaviour

Installing the larger propellers introduced new challenges.

Initially, the quadcopter exhibited:

- Oscillations during throttle increase
- Continuous oscillations during aggressive roll and pitch
- Slow recovery after rapid stick reversals
- Increased vibration reaching the MPU6050

The original PID values were no longer suitable due to the increased thrust and rotational inertia.

---

# Engineering Improvements

To adapt the flight controller for the larger propellers, several improvements were implemented.

Mechanical Improvements:

- Propeller balancing
- ESC calibration
- Frame balancing
- Vibration reduction

Software Improvements:

- Roll/Pitch proportional gain reduced
- Roll/Pitch derivative gain increased
- Multiple flight-test iterations
- Continuous PID optimisation

---

# Final Results

After optimisation, the 10×4.5 configuration achieved:

- Stable hover
- Smooth small stick response
- Stable medium stick manoeuvres
- Rapid recovery after aggressive manoeuvres
- No continuous oscillations
- Excellent controllability

---

# Observations

The experiments demonstrated that simply replacing propellers is not sufficient.

Larger propellers significantly change the dynamics of the aircraft.

Increasing propeller diameter results in:

- Greater thrust
- Increased rotational inertia
- Different damping characteristics
- Different PID requirements

Mechanical improvements should always be completed before modifying PID values.

---

# Conclusion

Both propeller configurations achieved excellent flight performance after optimisation.

The 8×4.5 configuration provides lightweight and responsive handling.

The 10×4.5 configuration provides greater lifting capability while maintaining excellent stability after systematic hardware improvements and PID tuning.

These experiments highlight the importance of adapting the flight controller to the physical characteristics of the propulsion system instead of relying on a single set of PID values.
