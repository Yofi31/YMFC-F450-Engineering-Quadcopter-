# Experiment 03 – Propeller Balancing

## Objective

The objective of this experiment was to reduce mechanical vibration generated by the propellers and improve the overall stability of the flight controller.

Excessive vibration introduces noise into the MPU6050 measurements, making accurate attitude estimation and PID control more difficult.

---

# Test Platform

Frame:
- F450 Quadcopter Frame

Flight Controller:
- Arduino r3

IMU:
- MPU6050

Motors:
- A2212 1000KV

Propellers:
- 8×4.5
- 10×4.5

---

# Why Propeller Balancing is Important

An unbalanced propeller creates centrifugal forces while rotating.

These forces produce:

- Increased vibration
- Noisy accelerometer readings
- Noisy gyroscope readings
- Reduced PID performance
- Oscillations during flight
- Poor hover stability

Even small imbalances become significant at several thousand RPM.

---

# Initial Observation

During early testing, noticeable vibrations were observed, particularly with the 10×4.5 propellers.

Although the flight controller remained functional, the increased vibration affected flight stability and made PID tuning more difficult.

---

# Balancing Method

Each propeller was inspected individually.

Small pieces of electrical tape were applied to the lighter blade until both blades reached an acceptable balance.

The balancing process was repeated until minimal vibration was observed during motor operation.

---

# Results

After balancing:

- Vibration noticeably reduced.
- Hover became smoother.
- MPU6050 sensor readings became more stable.
- PID tuning became more predictable.
- Recovery after aggressive manoeuvres improved.

---

# Observations

Although propeller balancing alone did not completely eliminate oscillations, it significantly reduced mechanical vibration reaching the flight controller.

This made subsequent PID tuning much more effective.

---

# Conclusion

Propeller balancing proved to be an essential mechanical improvement before software tuning.

Reducing vibration improved sensor accuracy, flight stability, and overall control performance.

This experiment demonstrated that hardware optimisation should always precede controller tuning.
